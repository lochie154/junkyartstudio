Artist Bio

Paloma Kop is an experimental multimedia artist, ambient musician, and creative technologist from the Catskill Mountains of upstate New York. Their practice incorporates electronic media, from analog video and synthesizers to digital systems and code, as well as various physical materials and processes. They are especially interested in feedback loops, recursion, self-organizing and emergent structures, patterns in nature, psychedelia, and embodied sensory experience.

They received a BFA in Visual Arts and Computer Science from Purchase College in 2014, and an MFA in Electronic Integrated Arts at Alfred University in 2020. Move Slow and Fix Things

As a practitioner of tech and electronic media, I try to counterbalance the cultural push towards compulsive productivity. I believe that technology should be used to enrich people's lives; corporate-owned social media networks, invasive data harvesting, and hype trains such as cryptocurrency and so-called "artificial intelligence" all serve to do the opposite. Under capitalism, companies are incentivized to manipulate and extract as much as possible.

DIY websites, decentralized networks, and creative communities inspire me to seek another path. A path that moves at a human speed, with space to breathe. One that values careful cultivation over explosive growth, thoughtfulness over conformity. Building and maintaining this website has become part of my exploration towards this path.
